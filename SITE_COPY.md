### Expanded explanations (one per symptom)

**Unexplained weight gain**
- Explanation: Estrogen plays a direct role in insulin sensitivity and where your body stores fat. As it shifts, your metabolism recalibrates — and the calorie math that used to work simply doesn't anymore. This is biology, not willpower.
- Stat: The most common first symptom in this age group — reported by 78% of women in Dr. Saxena's practice.

**Fatigue that sleep doesn't fix**
- Explanation: Hormonal fatigue is categorically different from tired. Estrogen and progesterone regulate your mitochondria — your cells' energy production. When they shift, no amount of sleep fully restores you. This is one of the first and most reversible symptoms with the right approach.
- Stat: 74% of women describe this as the symptom that finally made them seek answers.

**Brain fog or memory lapses**
- Explanation: Estrogen receptors line the hippocampus — the brain's memory center. When estrogen fluctuates, so does recall, word retrieval, and focus. This is not early dementia. It is almost certainly hormonal, and in most cases, highly reversible.
- Stat: Reported by 62% — and almost universally dismissed by conventional providers.

**Sleep disruption — waking at 3am**
- Explanation: Progesterone is your primary sleep hormone. As it declines in perimenopause, the architecture of your sleep changes — you fall asleep but can't stay there. The 3am wake-up is a hormonal signature, not anxiety.
- Stat: 61% of women in this age group report this specific pattern.

**Mood changes or new irritability**
- Explanation: Estrogen modulates serotonin and dopamine — your brain's primary mood regulators. Fluctuating estrogen creates a neurochemical instability that looks like irritability, low frustration tolerance, or sadness. It is not a character change. It is chemistry.
- Stat: 57% report mood changes as the symptom they feel most embarrassed to mention.

**Hair thinning or texture change**
- Explanation: Hormonal hair changes are driven by the ratio of estrogen to androgens, and by thyroid hormones that are sensitive to the same hormonal shifts. Hair follicles have estrogen receptors. When estrogen declines, follicle cycling changes. This is addressable — but requires the right diagnosis first.
- Stat: 44% — and almost always appears 6–12 months after the hormonal shift begins.

**Irregular or changing periods**
- Explanation: Cycle irregularity is often the body's first visible signal of perimenopause — years before periods stop. Shorter cycles, heavier flows, missed months: these are the ovary's way of communicating that the hormonal conversation has changed.
- Stat: 42% notice cycle changes 2–8 years before conventional menopause diagnosis.

**Hot flashes or night sweats**
- Explanation: Driven by estrogen's effect on the hypothalamus — your brain's thermostat. Vasomotor symptoms are one of the most studied and most treatable aspects of hormonal transition. If this is your primary complaint, you have excellent options.
- Stat: 41% — often the last symptom to appear, not the first, despite being the most discussed.

**Frozen shoulder or joint stiffness**
- Explanation: Estrogen has direct anti-inflammatory effects on joint tissue. As it declines, synovial fluid changes and tendon flexibility decreases. Frozen shoulder affects 10–17% of perimenopausal women and is almost never connected to hormones by a conventional provider. Dr. Saxena addresses this directly.
- Stat: One of the most validating moments in the program — women who've had this for years finally have an answer.

**Low libido**
- Explanation: Testosterone — yes, women produce it — governs libido, motivation, and a sense of vitality. It declines across the 40s alongside estrogen. This is physiological, not psychological, and it responds well to the right clinical approach.
- Stat: 35% report this but only 8% bring it up with their doctor unprompted.

**New or worsening anxiety**
- Explanation: GABA, the brain's calming neurotransmitter, is directly potentiated by progesterone. As progesterone declines, so does your nervous system's baseline calm. Anxiety that appears or worsens in your 40s — especially around your cycle — is almost always hormonal in origin.
- Stat: 33% — frequently misdiagnosed as a primary anxiety disorder and treated with SSRIs alone.

**Feeling like yourself — but off**
- Explanation: This is the symptom that is hardest to name but easiest to recognize. You function. You manage. But something that used to feel effortless now takes effort. You are not depressed. You are not 'just stressed.' You are hormonally transitioning — and nobody gave you a map.
- Stat: 69% of women describe this as their truest symptom — and the one they were most afraid to say out loud.

## FAQ

- **Eyebrow:** QUESTIONS
- **Headline:** The things people actually ask.

### 1. Is this a course or a live program?
Live. Three sessions on Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday — 75 minutes each on Zoom. 25 minutes of Shilpa teaching, 50 minutes of live Q&A and workshop. It may extend to 90 minutes when the energy calls for it. This is not a course. It's a conversation.

### 2. Will this program push BHRT on me?
No. The framework — Define Need, Assess Risk, Create Solution — exists precisely to ensure you don't end up with a solution before you understand your situation. Dr. Saxena covers the full spectrum: lifestyle interventions, bioidentical hormones, conventional pathways. Many women leave the boot camp choosing lifestyle changes first. Some choose BHRT. Some choose a combination. You get the information to make the call.

### 3. My OB-GYN already told me I don't need hormones. What's the point of this?
The boot camp is educational, not clinical. You won't receive a prescription this weekend. What you will receive is the evidence and the framework to have a far more informed conversation with any provider — including yours. You leave with a 1–2 page Patient Advocacy Document built around your specific story, your labs, and your questions. Many women find this changes the dynamic entirely.

### 4. Will you tell me what labs to get?
Yes. Day 2 walks through the recommended lab panel — what each marker tells you, how to interpret results, and how to ask your provider for them. Your Patient Advocacy Document ends up including a personalized Labs Recommended section based on your intake. Lab recommendations are educational; ordering and interpretation require a licensed provider in your state.

### 5. How is this different from a YouTube video or a $349 course?
Courses are monologues. This is a dialogue — with Shilpa, with your cohort, with your own story at the center. A pre-recorded 15-hour course cannot ask you follow-up questions. A 2-page document built around your specific answers can walk into your next appointment with you.

### 6. I'm in my late 30s. Is this program for me?
Perimenopause can begin as early as 35, and the hormonal shifts that precede it even earlier. If you're experiencing unexplained symptoms, the question isn't your age — it's your symptom profile and your hormone levels. Many of the women who've benefited most were in their late 30s and had been told they were 'too young' for what they were experiencing.

### 7. What is Coach Kai? Is an AI giving me medical advice?
Coach Kai is an AI assistant trained on Dr. Saxena's educational framework. Every clinical response is reviewed by a physician or nurse before it reaches you. Coach Kai is available on WhatsApp and in your browser — whichever you're most comfortable with. Intake is open now; clinical Q&A opens one week before your boot camp begins. Coach Kai does not diagnose or prescribe.

### 8. I'm in India. Can I attend?
Yes. The India cohort runs Tuesday / Wednesday / Thursday at 8:30–9:45 PM IST. Participants in India receive educational guidance and, where relevant, referrals to trained practitioners in your region. Our program is educational, not medical advice.

### 9. I'm a Forum Health patient — do I get a discount?
Yes — $50 off. The discount code is sent through Forum's patient communication channels. Tick the Forum Health patient box on the registration form and your discount applies at checkout.

### 10. How much time does this require?
Three 75-minute live sessions on Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday. Plus ~15 minutes of pre-program intake with Coach Kai spread across the week before. Designed for busy women — Dr. Saxena has run group programs for 20 years and knows that overwhelm is the enemy of action.

### 11. What happens after the boot camp?
